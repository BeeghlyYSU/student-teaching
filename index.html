The Student Teaching Experience

The Beeghly College of Liberal Arts, Social Sciences, and Education’s student teaching experience plays a significant role in teacher education candidates’ development as professional educators.

  • The student teaching experience requires a substantial time commitment, 16 weeks following the respective district’s teacher hours, with candidates considered as quasi staff members insofar as district policy, classroom instruction, and school building expectations are concerned. Candidates’ failure to comply with district policy and procedures may subject them to reprimands and/or removal from the student teaching experience.

  • The purpose for the student teaching experience is to learn about teaching. Student teacher candidates should have ample opportunity to engage in reflective activities, and assess his/her teacher behaviors and their consequences. All learning situations bring opportunities for success and failure; and student teaching is no exception. The student teaching experience provides candidates the opportunity to collaborate with teaching professionals, further refine their teaching style, increase their grade level content knowledge, and begin to establish classroom management and discipline. This occurs through candidates’ progressively teaching an instructional period at a time and building to a full day of teaching for multiple, consecutive weeks, for multiple instructional periods with units of instruction aligned with professional and state academic content standards. Cooperating teachers and university supervisors play an integral part in candidates’ professional development through mentoring, observation and providing constructive feedback.

  • In order to participate in the student teaching experience, all student teaching candidates are screened for eligibility and must meet specific course and grade point average prerequisites, course prerequisites with a recommendation to move to student teaching, and passage of the candidate’s respective OAE licensure exam(s). Then, at the conclusion of the experience, candidates ‘student teaching development is reviewed for a determination to endorse the teacher candidate for a teaching license.

We acknowledge and thank our school district partners for their support and commitment to providing instructional guidance and opportunities and mentoring during this important experience which guides our teacher candidates towards successful practice and meaningful careers.

Co-Teaching

The purpose of the student teaching experience is to learn about teaching. The Teacher Candidate should have ample opportunity to develop and practice the knowledge and skills required in the Ohio Standards for the Teaching Professions and engage in reflective activities to assess his/her teacher behaviors and their consequences. Therefore, the Beeghly College of Liberal Arts, Social Scienes, and Education recommends cooperating teachers mentor our Teacher Candidates by incorporating a co-teaching model during the student teaching experience. This approach will allow the Teacher Candidate to have a gradual increase in responsibilities with consistent monitoring and feedback from the cooperating teacher without the cooperating teacher giving up his/her classroom.
